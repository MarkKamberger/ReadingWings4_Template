Team Cooperation Goal
1. What effect does his missing lunch bag have on Ramon?
2. What is Ramon's main problem in the story?
3. What happens before Jack tells Emily and Ramon about the paint?
a. Ramon smells salami on Jack's breath.
b. They see him wiping mustard on a napkin.
c. They see an empty lunch bag on his desk.
d. The ask Jack about a stain on his shirt.
4. You can conclude from the text that Ramon and Emily -
a. think Jack is innocent.
b. believe Jack stole the sandwich.
c. are playing hooky.
d. think math class is the best of all.
Teacher Procedure
Team Cooperation Goal
  • Preview Team Talk questions. Point out that the answer to the Write-On question should be written individually after discussion.
  • Ask students to underline key words and phrases in the Write-On question. Model if necessary. Ask questions to guide students' reflection as they determine the meaning of the question.
  • Ask students to tell what key words or phrases they underlined and to state the Write-On question in their own words.
  • Randomly assign team leaders on day 1. Remind students that team leaders will make sure their teams:
    • - understand the role cards for discussion,
    - discuss strategy use,
    - prepare for Think-and-Connect discussion (test questions on the assessment day), and
    - write and discuss the answers to the Write-On question (except on assessment day).
